Dietary Advice for Alzheimer's Patients

Discover how proper nutrition can support brain health and help manage Alzheimer's symptoms.

Brain-Boosting Foods

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
  • Berries (blueberries, strawberries)
  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
  • Nuts and seeds (walnuts, flaxseeds)
  • Whole grains
  • Avocados

Foods to Limit

Use Caution With:

  • Processed foods
  • Added sugars
  • Excessive salt
  • Saturated fats
  • Alcohol

Hydration Guide

Recommended Beverages:

  • Water (8 glasses daily)
  • Green tea
  • Fresh fruit juices
  • Coconut water

Weekly Meal Plan Example

Breakfast Options

  • Oatmeal with berries and nuts
  • Greek yogurt parfait
  • Whole grain toast with avocado

Lunch Options

  • Grilled salmon salad
  • Mediterranean quinoa bowl
  • Turkey and avocado wrap

Dinner Options

  • Baked fish with roasted vegetables
  • Chicken stir-fry with brown rice
  • Lentil soup with whole grain bread

Brain-Boosting Smoothie

Blend: 1 cup berries, 1 banana, 1 tbsp flaxseeds, spinach, almond milk

Salmon Power Bowl

Combine: Grilled salmon, quinoa, vegetables, avocado

Diet and Brain Health

Research shows that a diet rich in omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and vitamins can help support cognitive function and potentially slow the progression of Alzheimer's disease.

Recommended Supplements

Always consult with your healthcare provider before starting any supplements:

  • Omega-3 fatty acids
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin B12
  • Vitamin E
